== Description ==

= Three people. Three problems. One thread. =

In 2021, Melanie came to me.
Marketing expert. Decades of experience. Someone who knows exactly when a tool is working -- and when it is just pretending to.

"I need webinars on WordPress," she said.
"Registration. Reminders. Tracking. Everything connected."

Everything was already there.
WordPress had the pieces: registration forms, video players, calendar links, thank-you pages, email tools.
Plugins for every single step.

But each one lived in its own world.
No handshake. No memory. No thread connecting them.

Juan spent weeks trying to bridge them manually.
The thank-you page opened without registration -- anyone could walk in.
No one knew who had signed up. Who had actually shown up. Who had leaned forward at the offer.
Without that, nothing could run on its own.
Every webinar needed a person holding it together.
Every follow-up was manual. Every sale, a coincidence.

I, Tobias, bought WebinarIgnition in 2022.
Not because it was finished.
Because for the first time, the thread was there.

One flow. Registration -> reminder -> webinar -> offer -> follow-up.
On your own site. With the plugins you already trust.

WooCommerce for checkout inside the webinar room.
Social login so signing up takes three seconds.
Polls, booking forms, countdown timers -- anything that keeps people engaged and present.
Webhooks that quietly tell your email marketing tool who showed up and who didn't.
Heatmaps, A/B tests, analytics -- because you want to know what worked, not just that something happened.

And underneath all of it: time.
Time to focus. Time to be present in the webinar, not behind it.
Time to sit with your audience -- week after week -- until they don't just know you.
Until they trust you.

That is what a webinar is, at its best.
Not a broadcast. A relationship, built slowly, in front of people who chose to show up.

---

Tom sold a video course.
Good product. Google Ads. Traffic.
500 people landed on his page.
Zero sales.

The page was beautiful.
Clear. Professional. Probably AI-optimized.
But no one bought.

Not because the offer was wrong.
Because trust takes longer than one page.

Tom stopped trying to sell on arrival.
Instead, he collected something simpler: registrations.
"Come to a live webinar. See who shows up. Hear what they actually ask."

The first session, he didn't sell anything.
He just listened.
What do these people worry about? Where are they stuck? What have they already tried?

And something shifted.
Not in his funnel. In the room.
People started answering each other's questions.
A stranger became a familiar voice.
Tom became someone they had spent an hour with -- not a logo on a landing page.

By the third session, he sold the course.
Not because he pitched harder.
Because the room had become a place people wanted to stay.

A webinar is not a funnel.
It is a space.
And people buy from spaces that feel safe.

---

Bastian sold leather goods online.
Handcrafted. Real quality. Not cheap.

He ran Google Ads directly to his WooCommerce product pages.
People clicked. People looked. People left.

The product page showed the price.
It showed the photos.
It did not show why this leather was different.
Why the stitching mattered. Why the smell of it told a story.
Why someone who buys it once, buys it again for life.

A product page has maybe thirty seconds.
Thirty seconds to explain something that took years to learn how to make.

Bastian started a webinar.
Not a sales pitch. A demonstration.
He showed how the leather ages. How it takes on character with use.
He answered questions live -- from people who were genuinely curious, not just browsing.

At the end, the WooCommerce checkout appeared right there -- inside the webinar room.
No new tab. No "find it on the shop." No moment where the feeling cools down.
Just: the product, the price, and a form that already knew his name.

They bought it in the room.
While the feeling was still warm.

Because they didn't just see a product anymore.
They understood it.
And understanding is the shortest path to trust.
And trust is the shortest path to yes.

= How is WebinarIgnition different from WebinarPress? =

WebinarIgnition is built around WooCommerce-first sales automation and multilingual audiences. WebinarPress focuses on English-only general lead generation. Key differences:

- Multilingual: WebinarIgnition supports 24 languages per-webinar -- registration pages, emails, thank-you pages, and the webinar room all adapt automatically. Your attendees register and convert in their native language. WebinarPress is English only -- non-native speakers take longer to understand your offer, and non-localized webinars convert less.
- In-room checkout: WebinarIgnition embeds WooCommerce checkout inside the webinar (Plus). WebinarPress redirects to a product page.
- AI content: WebinarIgnition generates registration pages and reminder emails in 24 languages on all plans. WebinarPress has no AI content wizard.
- Evergreen in free: WebinarIgnition includes automated and evergreen webinars in the free version. WebinarPress locks automated webinars to Pro.
- Shortcodes anywhere: Registration forms work in footers, sidebars, and popups, unlimited times on the same page (since 4.08). WebinarPress shortcodes are page-only.
- 1-click registration: WebinarIgnition sends personalized one-click registration links via email. WebinarPress does not.
- 100ms live video: Built-in 100ms video for up to 20,000 participants (Plus). WebinarPress relies on external Zoom/YouTube.
- Active development: WebinarIgnition ships major features regularly (see changelog). WebinarPress releases are mostly security patches since 2022.
